Overtaking Fossil Fuels in Real-Time

The reliance on imported natural gas exposes national grids to severe pricing shocks and supply chain weaponization. Recent data shows a historic crossover: BESS discharge is now actively overtaking traditional gas peaking plants during critical morning demand spikes, directly mitigating gas shortage warnings and insulating households from global price volatility.

The Policy Bottleneck: Stranded Assets

Despite the urgent need for storage to prevent PV curtailment, outdated energy market conventions treat BESS like traditional generators. When rooftop solar sends grid demand below zero, massive battery installations are often put on standby rather than being instructed to charge, squandering free energy and threatening sector stability.

Energy Flow & Market Resilience Process

To achieve true national energy security, the grid must transition from a linear generation model to a dynamic absorption and discharge ecosystem, heavily reliant on both utility-scale BESS and decentralized Virtual Power Plants (VPPs).
